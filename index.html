The Problem

Investigators are drowning in footage

A single incident can mean dozens of cameras, days of manual review, and critical leads going cold.

Status Quo

Why existing approaches fail

Human fatigue

Attention drops after 20 minutes. Critical frames get missed. It's biology, not laziness.

Face recognition fails

40–60% of real CCTV has no usable face. Hoods, masks, rear views, low resolution.

No way to search

You can't type "man in red jacket near gate 3" into any existing VMS. You just scrub and hope.

The Solution

LocaTORs turns passive CCTV footage
into a searchable evidence base

Type a description. Upload a photo. Or both.
Get ranked results across every camera in under a second.

How It Works

Three steps

1

Ingest

Upload footage. YOLOv8 detects every person. CLIP and OSNet generate searchable embeddings. FAISS indexes them.

2

Search + Detect

Text query, photo upload, or both. Ranked matches in <1s. X-CLIP flags 11 suspicious activity types automatically.

3

Track + Report

Cross-camera timeline stitched automatically. One-click PDF with evidence frames, trajectory, and timestamps.

Multi-Modal Search

Three ways to find anyone

Text search

Describe the suspect in plain language. CLIP matches visual semantics across all footage.

"man in red jacket near gate 3"

Photo search

Upload a reference image. OSNet matches by full body — works from behind, in crowds, with masks.

suspect.jpg → 47 matches

Hybrid search

Photo + description together. Weighted fusion of identity and context for highest precision.

photo + "carrying a blue bag"

Architecture

The stack

  • Detection YOLOv8 — real-time person detection
  • Re-ID OSNet — full-body re-identification (512-d)
  • Search CLIP ViT-B/32 — text-to-image matching
  • Activity X-CLIP — zero-shot video activity recognition
  • Index FAISS — sub-second vector similarity search

All pretrained models — zero training data required

Open-source — PyTorch, HuggingFace, no vendor lock-in

GPU-ready — CUDA acceleration with CPU fallback

Works with any camera — standard video formats, no hardware changes
